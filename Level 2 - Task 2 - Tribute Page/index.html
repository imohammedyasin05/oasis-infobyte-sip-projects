In Memory of the Missile Man of India

Dr. APJ Abdul Kalam

1931 — 2015

The Legend

Avul Pakir Jainulabdeen Abdul Kalam was an Indian aerospace scientist and statesman who served as the 11th President of India from 2002 to 2007. Born and raised in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu, he studied physics and aerospace engineering.

He spent the next four decades as a scientist and science administrator, mainly at the DRDO and ISRO, and was intimately involved in India's civilian space programme and military missile development efforts.

Life's Journey

1931

Born in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu, to a modest family.

1960

Joined DRDO as a scientist after graduating from Madras Institute of Technology.

1969

Transferred to ISRO, project director of India's first Satellite Launch Vehicle (SLV-III).

2002

Elected as the 11th President of India with massive public support.

2015

Passed away while doing what he loved most—delivering a lecture to students at IIM Shillong.
